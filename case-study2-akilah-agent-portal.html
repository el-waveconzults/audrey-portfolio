THE WHAT

The Akilaah Agent Portal is a digital platform designed to simplify and enhance the management of cooperative organizations and their members. Designed with simplicity and usability in mind, the portal reduces onboarding times, enhances operational efficiency, and fosters trust among agents, cooperatives, and members. It represents a transformative step in digitizing cooperative management, particularly in addressing the unique needs of its users.

THE CHALLENGE

Before the launch of the Agent Portal, the management of cooperatives and their members at Akilaah was fraught with inefficiencies and frustrations. Agents struggled to juggle fragmented systems, manual processes, and disorganized workflows, often leading to delays and errors. However, the challenges extended beyond the agents:

  • For Members: Members faced long onboarding times, inconsistent communication, and a lack of clarity regarding their registration and financial transactions. This created a sense of mistrust and a perception that the system was outdated and unreliable.
  • For Cooperatives: Cooperative managers struggled to maintain accurate records of their members, financial contributions, and overall performance. The lack of a centralized system often led to disputes, operational bottlenecks, and diminished trust in the process.

This disjointed approach hampered Akilaah’s ability to deliver on its promise of seamless cooperative management. A solution was urgently needed to unify and streamline operations for all stakeholders involved.

RESEARCH AND DISCOVERY

Research and Discovery

To better understand and solve the challenges faced, I and my team conducted:

  • Interviews with Agents: Identified pain points in their daily workflows, such as time-consuming onboarding processes and difficulty tracking commissions.
  • Feedback from Cooperative Managers Revealed the need for accurate and up-to-date information on members and contributions.
  • Surveys with Members: Highlighted frustrations with delayed registrations and lack of transparency in their financial activities.

KEY INSIGHTS

  1. 85% of users preferred mobile access over desktop due to convenience.
  2. Members highlighted the need for clear navigation, real-time updates, and visual feedback on financial trends.
  3. Younger members (aged 25–40) expressed a preference for modern, minimalist designs, while older users valued straightforward functionality.

DESIGN PROCESS

Wireframing And Prototyping

  • Mapped user journeys for each module (Dashboard, Cooperatives, Members, Finance) to ensure workflows were efficient and intuitive.

Visual Design

  • Focused on creating a clean, minimalistic interface that emphasized clarity and usability, ensuring accessibility for users with varying technical proficiency.

User Testing

  • Conducted iterative usability tests with agents and cooperative managers, leading to refinements such as clearer navigation labels.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

  1. Empathy Drives Design: Deep engagement with agents, members, and cooperative managers ensured the portal addressed real needs.
  2. Simplification Is Key: Focusing on intuitive workflows and minimal complexity made the portal accessible to users with varying tech skills.
  3. Feedback Matters: Iterative testing allowed for refinements that significantly improved usability and adoption.
  4. Transparency Builds Trust: Providing real-time updates and accurate financial tracking fostered trust among all stakeholders.
