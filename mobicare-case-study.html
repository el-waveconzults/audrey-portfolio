Mobi Care

Telemedicine App

MobiCare allows patients to receive treatment and consultations remotely, using technology such as video conferencing, phone calls, and messaging.

UX Case Study

Duration:

12th October - 18th December 2021

Target Audience

There are a number of potential target
audiences for the MobiCare app. They
include;

  • Patients who want to save time and money by avoiding
    unnecessary in-person visits to the doctor.
  • Patients who need immediate medical attention but
    can't get to a hospital or clinic right away.
  • Patients with mobility issues or disabilities that make it
    difficult for them to visit a medical facility in person.

Empathy Map

Pain Points
  1. Pain Point 1
    Facilities are insufficient as a result of overcrowding of hospitals.
  2. Pain Point 2
    Absence of specialists to attend to patients with special cases.
  3. Pain Point 3
    Too many patients files to deal with and sort out.
  4. Pain Point 4
    Slow response and long queues.

Product Overview

MobiCare allows patients to access healthcare services from
the comfort of their own homes, eliminating the need to
physically visit a doctor’s office or hospital.
MobiCare can help reduce healthcare costs by eliminating
the need for patients to travel to receive treatment, and by
reducing the need for in-person visits, which can be more
expensive than virtual visits.

Problem Statement

In Nigeria, overcrowding in hospitals is a major issue
caused by insufficient health facilities relative to the
population. This problem makes individuals and even
healthcare professionals frustrated. People complain
about delayed consultations and not receiving prompt
assistance in emergency situations. Hence, patients want
a swift and hitch-free consultation from medical
practitioners/specialists.

Goals

The Goal is to create a telemedicine app that can help
patients feel more involved in their own care by providing
them with easy access to their healthcare providers and
enabling them to communicate with their healthcare
team from the comfort of their own home. This will
drastically reduce wait time.

User Research

User interviews were conducted as part of the user research. Participants were persons who frequently visited hospitals; they responded to a series of questions and shared unpleasant hospital experiences. I was able to learn more about the users and their problems through the user interviews. In order to meet user expectations and address significant issues, the insights gained from the user interviews were included into the designs.

Competitive Analysis

To assess the positives and downsides of some competing platforms in that industry, competitive audit is conducted. Two physical hospitals (1 and 2) and one telemedicine app were studied to produce the best app possible, with a focus on the procedures required to see a doctor among other things.

Unique Features

  • Virtual Consultation Capabilities (Chat and Video)
  • Healthcare Professionals Booking
  • Online Pharmacy
  • Laboratory Test Booking
  • Accurate Patient History Documentation

Ruth is a 23-year-old working professional who

it provides. She can schedule virtual appointments

get to the doctor’s office.

Key Takeaways

One key lesson I learned from working on this UX case study as a UX/UI designer is the importance of considering the needs and preferences of different user groups.

Additionally, research is important as it helps us understand the various context in which telemedicine may be used, such as in a hospital setting, at home, or on the go, and to design the user experience accordingly. Overall, designing a successful telemedicine platform requires a deep understanding of the needs and motivations of the target users.

Future Roadmap

  • Review feedback from user testing and make any necessary changes to the telemedicine interfaces based on the results.
  • Measure the usage and effectiveness of the telemedicine platform, gathering feedback and making improvements as needed.
  • Continuously test and refine the design of the telemedicine platform to ensure it meets the needs of users and stakeholders over time.
